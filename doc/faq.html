F.A.Q.

Why is the game called "Pictorial Consequences"?
The real life version of this game is actually a sequel to another real life paper game called "Consequences". Consequences is a very similar game.

Players sit in a circle and each is given a long, thin piece of paper. Each player begins by writing a male name at the top of their piece of paper. This male name is generally a one of their friends, a celebrity or a figure from myth or legend. The player then folds the writing so that the next player cannot see the name and hands it to the next player in the circle.

The next player then writes "Met " and a female name. They then also fold over the paper over again so their writing cannot be seen, and pass it on again.

The next player then writes "At " and a location. They then fold over the paper and pass it on.

The next player then writes "He said " and a whatever they would like the male character to have said. They then fold over the paper and pass it on.

The next player then writes "So she said " and a comment they would like the female character to have said. They then fold over the paper and pass it on.

The next player then writes "So he " and an action for male character to do. They then fold over the paper and pass it on.

The next player then writes "And she " and an action for female character. They then fold over the paper and pass it on.

The next player then writes "And the consequence of all this was " and the consequence of the whole story.

That's where the game ends. Everybody unfurls a piece and laughs at the silliness of it all.

So an example of a game might be:

The abominable snowman
Met Judi Dench
At the restaurant at the end of the universe.
He said "Hey honey, why don't you buy me a kebab?"
So she said "Oh, a wise guy, eh?"
So he went down to the local shops to buy safety pins
And she ignored the spider sitting on his head.
And the consequence of all this was that there were 7 less birds flying South that year.
